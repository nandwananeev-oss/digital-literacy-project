# ***UPI/ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD***



A common form of cybercrime in India today is UPI-based online payment fraud. This type of fraud usually targets students and individuals who frequently use digital payment apps.



In a typical scenario, the fraud begins with the victim receiving a phone call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or a customer. The fraudster may claim that money needs to be transferred or that there is an issue with the victim’s account. They then send a payment request through a UPI app and instruct the victim to approve it, often under pressure or urgency.



Many users mistakenly believe that accepting a request will receive money, when in reality it results in money being debited from their account. In some cases, scammers also send fake links or QR codes that redirect users to fraudulent payment pages.



The primary targets of such frauds are students and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment systems. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes compromise of personal information.



This type of cybercrime highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment platforms. Understanding how these scams work can help individuals avoid becoming victims.
